Uliana Popov. PG&E Power Outages

Power Outages in North California

Motivation

Since 2007 California has a fire season [1]. PG&E, power provider to most of California, was found responsible for more than 80% of the state's major wildfires. Thus fire preventing power shutoffs were happening this year. According to PG&E chief executive Bill Johnson the outages might continue for a decade [2]. There are 5.4 million PG&E electric customers. During the blackouts PG&E's website crashed several times, call center were overloaded [3]. Nor PG&E nor people were prepared for such massive shutoffs. .

The goal of my project is to help people and organizations to prepare for the next fire season.

The Data

Simon Willison has been scraping data from PG&E's website last few months. The data lives in his github repository. Each snapshot contains: Location, Number of Customers, Cause, Time Stamp…

Findings

  • 3.7 million (out of 5.4 million) customers were affected
  • The longest shutoff lasted 383 hours (almost 16 days)
  • Average fire outage lasts 4 times longer than “regular” outage, 43.8 vs 10.7 hours
  • More than half (55%) fire outages started during the night, between 10pm and 7am.
